Welcome to the survey! Many thanks for giving me some of your precious time.

In this study, we investigate what could explain reducing fertility rates in Western countries like Germany, Italy, Spain, Belgium, etc.

The world has undergone some important changes, including the number of children families have in the West. However, the latter observation is worrisome. According to the current demographic trajectory of the West, there are not enough children being born to keep up with the ageing population. If fertility rates do not increase, the retirement system will need to undergo drastic changes, which may not be beneficial to the current working generation.

Although we observe a decreasing trend in the number of children a household has, there is one crucial observation that warrants more attention: notably, the Fertility Gap.

The latter refers to the gap between the number of children a household has and the number of children it wishes to have. Essentially, despite the desire to have more children, the wish does not materialise. The focus of the study is to examine what participants consider the most significant factors.

Your contribution is very valuable and can help us in further research in the field of Family Economics, and actually tackling the Fertility Gap .
